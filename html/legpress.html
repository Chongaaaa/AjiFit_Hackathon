    • Leg Press

    Back to AjiFit Exercise

    Overview:

    The leg press is a popular resistance exercise that primarily targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes.
    It involves pushing a weighted platform away from the body using the legs while seated on a leg press machine.

    Difficulty Level:

    • Beginners: Start with lighter weights and focus on mastering proper form. Aim for 2-3 sets of 10-12 repetitions.
    • Intermediate: Gradually increase the weight lifted as you become stronger. Use moderate weights and aim for 3-4 sets of 8-10 repetitions.
    • Advanced: Lift heavier weights to challenge your strength and muscle endurance. Aim for 4-5 sets of 6-8 repetitions.

    Tips for Proper Form:

    1. Sit comfortably on the leg press machine with your back flat against the backrest.
    2. Place your feet shoulder-width apart on the foot platform, with toes pointed slightly outward.
    3. Engage your core muscles and firmly press your back against the backrest throughout the exercise.
    4. Push the weight away from your body by extending your legs, keeping your knees in line with your toes.
    5. Lower the weight back down with control, allowing your knees to bend to approximately 90 degrees.
    6. Avoid locking out your knees at the top of the movement and keep the motion smooth and controlled.
    7. Adjust the seat and foot position to ensure proper alignment and range of motion for your body.

    Progression:

    To progress in the leg press exercise:

    • Increase weight: Gradually increase the weight lifted as you get stronger. This will help continue to challenge your muscles and stimulate growth.
    • Adjust foot position: Experiment with different foot positions on the foot platform, such as wider or narrower stances, to target different muscles and increase difficulty.
    • Change tempo: Incorporate tempo variations, such as slowing down the eccentric (lowering) phase of the movement, to increase time under tension and muscle activation.
    • Try single-leg variations: Progress to single-leg leg presses to further challenge balance, stability, and unilateral leg strength.
