    • Tricep Dip

    Back to AjiFit Exercise

    Overview:

    Tricep bench dips are a compound bodyweight exercise that primarily targets the triceps brachii muscles, along with engaging the chest and shoulders to a lesser extent.
    They involve lowering and raising your body using a stable surface, such as a bench or chair, to work the triceps effectively.

    Difficulty Level:

    • Beginners: Start with bench dips using your body weight only. Aim for 2-3 sets of 10-12 repetitions.
    • Intermediate: Progress to more challenging variations by elevating your feet on another bench or using a dip station. Aim for 3-4 sets of 8-10 repetitions.
    • Advanced: Increase the intensity by adding weight with a weighted vest or holding a dumbbell between your feet. Aim for 4-5 sets of 6-8 repetitions.

    Tips for Proper Form:

    1. Place your hands shoulder-width apart on the edge of a bench or chair, fingers facing forward.
    2. Extend your legs out in front of you with your heels on the ground or elevated on another bench.
    3. Lower your body by bending your elbows until your upper arms are parallel to the ground.
    4. Keep your back close to the bench and your elbows pointing backward, not flaring out to the sides.
    5. Push through your palms to straighten your arms and return to the starting position, fully extending your elbows.
    6. Engage your core muscles throughout the exercise to maintain stability and prevent excessive arching of the lower back.
    7. Focus on controlled movements and avoid using momentum to lift your body.

    Progression:

    To progress in tricep bench dips:

    • Increased resistance: Gradually increase the resistance by adding weight with a weighted vest, placing a weight plate on your lap, or holding a dumbbell between your feet.
    • Elevate feet: Progress to more advanced variations by elevating your feet on another bench or platform. This increases the workload on your triceps and makes the exercise more challenging.
    • Change tempo: Experiment with different tempos, such as slowing down the lowering phase (eccentric) or adding pauses at the bottom of the movement, to increase time under tension and muscle activation.
    • Try single-arm dips: Progress to single-arm bench dips to further challenge your triceps and core stability. Perform the exercise with one arm while keeping the other arm raised or extended.
