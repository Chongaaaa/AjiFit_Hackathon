    • Bench Press

    Back to AjiFit Exercise

    Overview:

    The bench press is a compound upper body exercise that primarily targets the chest muscles (pectoralis major), front deltoids, and triceps.
    It involves lying flat on a bench and pressing a weight upwards from the chest, then lowering it back down to complete one repetition.

    Difficulty Level:

    • Beginners: Start with lighter weights or use the barbell without added weight plates. Aim for 2-3 sets of 8-12 repetitions.
    • Intermediate: Gradually increase the weight lifted as you become stronger. Use moderate weights and aim for 3-4 sets of 6-10 repetitions.
    • Advanced: Lift heavier weights to challenge your strength and muscle endurance. Aim for 4-5 sets of 4-8 repetitions.

    Tips for Proper Form:

    1. Position yourself on a flat bench with your feet firmly planted on the ground.
    2. Grasp the barbell with an overhand grip slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
    3. Lower the barbell towards your chest in a controlled manner, keeping your elbows tucked close to your body.
    4. Press the barbell upwards in a straight line, fully extending your arms without locking out your elbows.
    5. Lower the barbell back down to your chest and repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
    6. Engage your core muscles throughout the exercise to stabilize your body and prevent excessive arching of the lower back.
    7. Use a spotter when lifting heavier weights to ensure safety and proper form.

    Progression:

    To progress in the bench press exercise:

    • Increase weight: Gradually increase the amount of weight lifted as you become stronger.
    • Adjust reps and sets: Modify the number of repetitions and sets to continue challenging your muscles.
    • Focus on form: Pay attention to proper form and technique to maximize muscle engagement and reduce the risk of injury.
    • Incorporate variations: Experiment with different bench press variations, such as incline bench press or close-grip bench press, to target muscles from different angles and prevent plateaus.
