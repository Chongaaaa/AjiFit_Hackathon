    • Russian Twist

    Back to AjiFit Exercise

    Overview:

    The Russian twist is a core-strengthening exercise that primarily targets the oblique muscles.
    It involves sitting on the floor with your knees bent and feet elevated, then rotating your torso from side to side.

    Difficulty Level:

    • Beginners: Start without using any weights or with a light weight. Aim for 2-3 sets of 10-15 repetitions per side.
    • Intermediate: Gradually increase the intensity of the exercise. Aim for 3-4 sets of 15-20 repetitions per side.
    • Advanced: Use a faster tempo or increase the range of motion to challenge your core muscles. Aim for 4-5 sets of 20-25 repetitions per side.

    Tips for Proper Form:

    1. Sit on the floor with your knees bent and feet elevated, balancing on your glutes.
    2. Keep your back straight and your core engaged throughout the exercise.
    3. Place your hands together in front of your chest or extend them straight out in front of you.
    4. Rotate your torso to one side, bringing your hands towards the floor beside your hip.
    5. Return to the starting position and then rotate to the other side, keeping your feet elevated off the floor.
    6. Continue alternating sides in a controlled manner, focusing on using your oblique muscles to perform the movement.
    7. Avoid using momentum or swinging your arms to complete the exercise. Keep the movement controlled and controlled.

    Progression:

    To progress in the Russian Twist exercise:

    • Increase repetitions: Gradually increase the number of repetitions performed per set.
    • Add weights: Hold a weight plate, dumbbell, or medicine ball to increase resistance.
    • Use a stability ball: Perform the exercise on a stability ball to increase the challenge to your core stability.
    • Experiment with tempo: Try slowing down the movement or adding pauses at different points in the exercise to increase time under tension.
