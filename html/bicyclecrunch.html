    • Bicycle Crunches

    Back to AjiFit Exercise

    Overview:

    Bicycle crunches are a core-strengthening exercise that targets the abdominal muscles, including the rectus abdominis and obliques.
    This exercise is performed by lying on your back, bringing your knees towards your chest, and alternately touching your elbows to the opposite knee in a pedaling motion, mimicking the movement of riding a bicycle.

    Difficulty Level:

    • Beginners: Start with a moderate pace and focus on proper form. Aim for 2-3 sets of 10-15 repetitions per side.
    • Intermediate: Increase the pace and intensity of the exercise by performing more repetitions or adding a pause at the top of each repetition. Aim for 3-4 sets of 15-20 repetitions per side.
    • Advanced: Perform the exercise at a faster pace with controlled movements and a full range of motion. Aim for 4-5 sets of 20-25 repetitions per side.

    Tips for Proper Form:

    1. Lie flat on your back with your hands behind your head, elbows pointing outwards.
    2. Bring your knees towards your chest and lift your shoulder blades off the ground.
    3. Extend one leg out straight while bringing the opposite elbow towards the opposite knee, twisting your torso.
    4. Switch sides in a fluid motion, as if pedaling a bicycle, while keeping your core engaged.
    5. Focus on exhaling as you crunch and inhaling as you return to the starting position.
    6. Avoid pulling on your neck or using momentum to complete the movement. Keep the movement controlled and engage your abdominal muscles throughout.

    Progression:

    To progress in the bicycle crunches exercise:

    • Increase repetitions: Gradually increase the number of repetitions performed per set.
    • Add weights: Hold a weight plate or dumbbell across your chest to increase resistance and challenge your abdominal muscles.
    • Experiment with tempo: Slow down the movement to increase time under tension and enhance muscle engagement.
    • Explore advanced variations: Try more challenging variations of bicycle crunches, such as twisting bicycle crunches or hanging bicycle crunches, to further challenge your core muscles.
