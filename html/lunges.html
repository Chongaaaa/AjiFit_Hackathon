    • Lunges

    Back to AjiFit Exercise

    Overview:

    Lunges are a versatile lower body exercise that targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves.
    It involves stepping forward or backward with one leg, lowering the body until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle, and then returning to the starting position.

    Difficulty Level:

    • Beginners: Start with bodyweight lunges or using light dumbbells for added resistance. Aim for 2-3 sets of 10-12 repetitions per leg.
    • Intermediate: Progress to performing lunges with moderate weights, such as holding a barbell on your shoulders or using heavier dumbbells. Aim for 3-4 sets of 8-10 repetitions per leg.
    • Advanced: Incorporate advanced lunge variations, such as walking lunges, reverse lunges, or Bulgarian split squats, with heavier weights to challenge your muscles further. Aim for 4-5 sets of 6-8 repetitions per leg.

    Tips for Proper Form:

    1. Stand tall with your feet hip-width apart.
    2. Take a step forward or backward with one leg, lowering your body until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle.
    3. Keep your front knee aligned with your ankle and avoid letting it extend past your toes.
    4. Engage your core muscles to stabilize your body and keep your torso upright.
    5. Push through your front heel to return to the starting position, driving your hips forward.
    6. Switch legs and repeat the movement on the opposite side.
    7. Focus on maintaining proper form and control throughout the exercise to maximize results and reduce the risk of injury.

    Progression:

    To progress in lunges exercise:

    • Increase weight: Gradually increase the weight lifted as you become stronger.
    • Adjust range of motion: Increase the depth of your lunges for a greater challenge, ensuring proper form is maintained.
    • Experiment with variations: Try different lunge variations, such as walking lunges, reverse lunges, or jumping lunges, to target muscles in different ways.
    • Incorporate plyometrics: Add plyometric lunges, such as switch lunges or jumping lunges, to improve power and explosiveness in your lower body.
